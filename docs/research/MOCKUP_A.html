Direction at a Glance
  • Minimum layout disruption — accordion shell, sidebar, and heading all unchanged
  • Titles replace dates as the primary identifier; researcher reads down the list, not into each session
  • Search is scoped per challenge — forces the user to commit to a challenge before searching
Strategy
Direction A exists because the brief's §6 Scope A constraint is a hard boundary — the accordion structure, sidebar, and top navigation must survive. The core §2 Problem 1 (no meaningful identity per attempt) is solved by a single, high-impact row change: the title column. The §4 primary metric (found in under 5 seconds) is served by scannability of that title at the list level — no click-in required for orientation. By preserving the accordion, this direction also has the lowest implementation risk and the fastest path to a shipped improvement.
