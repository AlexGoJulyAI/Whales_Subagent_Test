Product Vision

Audible is a subscription audiobook and podcast platform. The Browse navigation is the primary content discovery entry point, organized into three columns: Get Started, Popular Lists, and Explore Audible.

This engagement adds a dedicated Narrators discovery path to the Popular Lists column — a new page giving narrator-loyal listeners a direct browsing entry point. Success = a Narrators page reachable from the Browse nav in one click, with functional filter chips and in-page narrator-name search.

2
Problem Statements
Problem
Audible's Popular Lists navigation surfaces discovery through content-level attributes (bestsellers, release date, editorial picks) but offers no narrator-based entry point. Narrator loyalty is a documented audiobook behavior — listeners repeatedly seek out specific voices — yet the current Browse nav does not support this discovery mode. Narrator-based browsing requires a manual search, not a supported browse path.
Framing Note
Client brief mentioned replacing "Series" in Popular Lists. Live extraction confirmed "Series" does not currently appear in Popular Lists (current items: Bestsellers, Coming Soon, New Releases, Best of the Year, Best of #BookTok). Correct framing: add "Narrators" to this column. Placement is a ★ CLIENT DEFERRED decision explored across design directions.

3
Target Users

Primary persona: Mid-to-high engagement Audible subscribers who have developed narrator preferences — they know whose voice they trust and arrive with intent to find more books by a narrator they love, or discover narrators in genres they enjoy.

Context: Web browser, desktop, discovery mode. Familiar with Audible's existing chip + list browse pattern from other pages.

Technical fluency: Average web user — no new interaction paradigm required.

Brand Identity

Logo: Audible wordmark + "an amazon company" lockup, top-left. Locked — do not modify.

Font: "Audible Sans" (Audible custom typeface), Arial fallback. No substitutions permitted.

Primary CTA: #ffa000 orange. Links: #0e5b9b.

Voice: Warm, discovery-oriented — "Find your next great listen." Concise labels (single words or short phrases for nav items).

10
Visual Design Language

Surface: White backgrounds (#ffffff), clean editorial structure, minimal chrome. Cover art is the primary visual — UI stays restrained so book covers dominate.

Typography: "Audible Sans" throughout — humanist sans-serif. Size hierarchy: 48px H1 (centered, 700wt) → 20px book title → 13–14px metadata. Weight distinguishes hierarchy.

Components: adbl-chip outline style (8px radius, 2px border, 32px height). bc-button pill CTAs (32px radius, orange fill). 232px square cover images. Result items: horizontal layout — cover left, metadata center, price/CTA right.

Motion: Standard browser hover color transitions only — no significant animation on existing Audible pages.
