What this changes — Direction B callout index
1 Ghost chapter numbers — enormous watermark numerals (01–04) positioned behind section titles on every onboarding screen. These are the first thing a client sees; they signal editorial confidence and orient parents without requiring them to read a step-counter.
2 Chapter dot navigation row — row of 4 dots at the bottom of each onboarding screen. Active dot is a pill (24px wide, sage). Completed dots are muted sage (opacity 0.4) and are tappable — parents can navigate backward to any completed chapter without losing progress. Future dots are light gray (#CBD5E1).

Strategy

Direction B exists because parents setting up SafeChat need to feel like authors of their family's settings, not subjects of a wizard. The chapter model gives the journey shape and structure they can navigate — reducing anxiety about "what's still left?" which directly serves Brief §4's primary metric by eliminating premature drop-off caused by uncertainty about remaining steps.

The non-linear chapter dots address §3's control-gap by letting parents revise any decision without restarting. This reinforces trust that settings are active and correct, the core confidence signal required before a parent hands the app to their child.

Why This Is the Primary Screen

Screen 3 concentrates both of Direction B's P0 elements at peak decision density. The ghost "03" anchors spatial confidence ("I'm in the third chapter — I'm almost done") while the parent makes individual per-topic decisions. Two completed dots to the left give visible evidence of progress; the active pill at position 3 confirms imminent completion.

The backward tappability of dots 1–2 is the key trust signal on this screen. The "← tap to revisit" hint below the dot row signals to the parent that their earlier choices (tier defaults from Ch.02) are not locked — they can revise without restarting. This directly addresses §3's parent control gap.

Spatial Summary — Direction B: Chapter Guide
Direction
B — Chapter Guide
Layout
Single-column screen with ghost chapter numbers as spatial anchors; chapter dot nav provides non-linear backward access. Two distinct zones: ghost number (top, z-index 0) + content (z-index 1) + dot nav (bottom).
Distinctive layout move
Ghost numerals (01–04) float behind section titles, making the journey's shape visible from any chapter. Parents always know their position in a 4-chapter sequence without reading progress text.
New / changed zones
Ghost number overlay (top zone, absolute positioned, z-index 0) · Chapter dot navigation row (bottom zone, above home indicator, centered)
Backward navigation
Completed chapter dots are interactive (44pt touch target). Tapping any completed dot navigates backward without resetting subsequent choices. State is preserved across chapters.
Ghost number appearance
Onboarding screens 1–4 only. Absent from Dashboard, Child Login, Child Chatbot. #E5E7EB · opacity 0.6 · 72px / weight 900 · DM Sans · absolute top-left behind content.
Proportional note
Phone frames rendered at ~57% of 390px baseline [estimated]. Ghost number at 72px maps to ~126px at 1× — approximately ⅓ of the full screen height, large enough to read instantly without obscuring title.
Design references
Calm app (sleek, minimal, intuitive, trustworthy) · DM Sans typeface · oklch-compatible tokens · soft elevation only (no harsh shadows)
P0 components
Ghost Chapter Number · Chapter Dot Navigation (both new, Direction B exclusive)
