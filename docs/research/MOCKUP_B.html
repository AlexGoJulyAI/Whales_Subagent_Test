Direction at a Glance
  • Scope A — sidebar, top navigation, and "Practice Sets" heading unchanged; only the accordion/table area is replaced
  • Challenge selector column is permanently visible — no accordion expand required to see challenge context or outcome summary
  • Attempt feed shows 2-line excerpt inline — the learner gets content orientation at the list level, not from clicking in
Strategy
Direction B exists because the accordion in Scope A imposes a hidden cost: to see challenge context, the user must expand it; to see attempt content, they must click in. These are two unnecessary navigation steps before any meaningful information is visible. The split-panel model eliminates both: challenge names and outcome summaries are permanently visible in the left column (addressing §4's secondary metric — identifying scenarios without opening the prompt), while the right column surfaces AI-generated titles and excerpts at the list level (addressing §4's primary metric — finding a specific conversation in under 5 seconds). By staying within Scope A, this direction has zero risk of disrupting the surrounding page structure.
