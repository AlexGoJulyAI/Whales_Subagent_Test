Direction at a Glance
  • Scope B — fixes the heading mismatch and replaces accordions with a flat tab strip; the deepest structural change of the four options
  • Global search above the tabs is the defining move — a researcher does not need to choose a challenge before searching; they type and results from all challenges appear
  • Attempt cards show 2-line excerpt inline — the learner reads content context at the list level, without any click-in required
Strategy
Direction C exists because §2 Problem 3 (challenge identity opacity) is not solved by richer rows alone — it requires putting the scenario name where the user's eye lands first: the navigation primitive. The accordion hides challenge identity behind a collapsed header; tabs expose it as a first-class label. §4's secondary metric (identifying challenge scenario without opening the prompt) is served directly by tab labels like "Self-Harm · 9 attempts." The §4 primary metric (found in under 5 seconds) is served most powerfully by global search: when a researcher doesn't remember which challenge a target attempt belongs to, they should not need to guess a tab before querying. Global search removes that constraint entirely.
