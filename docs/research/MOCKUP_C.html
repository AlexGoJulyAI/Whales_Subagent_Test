Distinctive layout move: Full-width hero search autofocused on page load — the first affordance a user sees IS the action they need to take.
New/changed zones: Nav (1), hero search (2), Popular Narrators chips (3), sticky filter bar (4).

Direction at a Glance
Full-width narrator-name search field dominates above fold — it IS the page’s first affordance
Popular Narrators chip row bridges name-knowers and explorers: clicking a chip populates the search
Sticky filter bar emerges on scroll — filter-after-browse rather than filter-then-browse
Strategy
Direction C exists because §3 identifies two user types — name-knowers and explorers — whose discovery modes conflict with a single chip or sidebar interface. A single hero search with Popular Narrator shortcuts serves both: name-knowers type immediately (autofocused field requires zero orientation), and explorers click a chip or scroll down (Popular Narrators are curated shortcuts to the most recognizable names). The §4 primary metric is satisfied by making the nav-to-page journey feel like arriving at the right tool rather than a generic list page.
Above the Fold
1st: H1 “Narrators” + hero search field — search is the primary action, page identity is clear
2nd: Popular Narrators chip row — curated shortcuts for explorers; social proof the catalog has well-known names
3rd: “or browse all narrators” divider — signals the list exists below for non-search users
