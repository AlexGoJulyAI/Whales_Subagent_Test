Product Vision

TreeHouse is an iOS mobile app that gives parents meaningful, granular control over how AI interacts with their children. Parents configure a safety policy across five dimensions — content moderation, behavioral monitoring, AI personality, academic integrity, and data privacy — and monitor outcomes through a dashboard. Children access a guided AI chatbot experience (SafeChat) operating within the guardrails their parent set.

TreeHouse is the parent-facing app brand. SafeChat is the AI feature embedded within it. The parent builds the container; the child lives in it.

Success: a parent completes the full onboarding configuration in under 5 minutes with confidence in their choices, and a child gets a natural, engaging AI experience that is transparently safer than the default — without feeling restricted or condescended to.

2
Problem Statements
Core Problem
Parents have no way to meaningfully configure how AI models interact with their children. Default AI systems are built for adults, apply no age-appropriate filtering, and provide zero visibility into what their child is doing or experiencing.
Secondary Problem
Most parental controls are blunt instruments — block everything or block nothing. Parents need nuanced, category-level control with transparent tradeoffs, and behavioral monitoring that detects patterns over time (not just individual bad messages).
Framing Note
The product is parent-led (configuration drives the experience) but child-retention-dependent. Both user views are equal-priority deliverables. If the child chatbot feels restrictive or babyish, the product fails even if the parent dashboard is perfect.

Target Users

Primary User A — Parent

A parent of a middle schooler (approx. ages 10–14). Not an AI expert. Motivated by safety, not control for its own sake. Arrives at onboarding slightly anxious — they know AI can be risky for kids but don't fully understand how. Their key emotional need is confidence: they want to leave setup feeling like they did the right thing for their family. Time-constrained (5 minutes is their threshold). Will abandon a flow that feels like reading a legal document.

Primary User B — Child (Middle Schooler)

Ages 10–14. Comfortable with smartphone interfaces. Will compare the experience immediately to ChatGPT or other AI tools they've seen. Key need: the product doesn't feel "babyish" or over-restricted. A child who feels condescended to disengages. They don't know about the parent configuration layer and should not be reminded of it constantly.

Visual Design Language

Aesthetic

Calm-inspired soft modernism. Deep navy surfaces for high-stakes screens (onboarding completion, alert states). Warm off-white light surfaces for content-heavy screens (dashboard, chat). Teal accent used sparingly as a trust signal — primary actions, active states, progress indicators.

Surface Treatment

Onboarding cards: White surface on #F7F5F1 background; 24px radius; soft shadow (0 4px 20px rgba(22,43,69,0.08)); full-bleed with no visible screen edge.

Dashboard: Light bg (#F7F5F1) with white cards floating above it. Tab-switched or scroll-based content for the three dashboard panels.

Dark screens (opener, confirmation): #162B45 deep navy with subtle top-to-bottom gradient toward a slightly lighter navy. White and teal text only.

Child chatbot: Ultra-clean white surface, minimal visual chrome. The conversation is the UI — no decorative elements. AI bubbles left-aligned, white + soft shadow. User bubbles right-aligned, teal fill + white text.

Typography

Nunito Bold/ExtraBold for display headings (onboarding titles, dashboard section heads). Nunito Regular/SemiBold for body and labels. Single typeface across the full scale — no second typeface. Line heights: 1.5–1.6 for body; 1.2–1.3 for display.

Motion

Onboarding card transitions: lateral swipe (left-to-right advance, right-to-left back) — 320ms ease-in-out. Dashboard: fade-in on load (250ms). Chat: message bubbles animate in from bottom (200ms, slight spring). Safety pills: tap → fill transition (180ms ease-out).

Iconography

SF Symbols (iOS-native) for system icons. Custom styled SVGs for safety category labels (matching client document emoji-adjacent icons: ❤️ self-harm, 🧪 drugs, 🍎 eating disorders, etc.) — rendered as styled SVG, not emoji.

Background Rule

Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) as a page background. #F7F5F1 is always the page; #FFFFFF is always a card surface floating above it.

User Tasks
Task 1 — Parent Onboarding
A parent needs to create an account, set up their child's profile, and complete all 5 sections of safety configuration in a single sitting — without needing to read a manual or ask for help on any setting.
Task 2 — Parent Dashboard: Act on an Alert
A parent who received a notification needs to open the app, understand what triggered the alert, read the relevant context, and decide whether to act — all within the first 30 seconds of opening the app.
Task 3 — Child Chatbot: Complete a Task
A child needs to ask the AI a question, receive a useful answer, and continue a multi-turn conversation — without the safety guardrails feeling like friction, interruption, or condescension during a normal interaction.
