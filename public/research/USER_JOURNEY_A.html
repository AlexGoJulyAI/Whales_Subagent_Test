Flow 1 — Find a specific conversation
Step 1Modified
User arrives at the Previous Conversations tab
A prominent search bar sits at the top of the page, with the three Challenge sections collapsed beneath it showing each challenge name and its attempt count.
↓
Step 2New
User types a keyword into the search bar
Challenge sections whose transcripts contain the keyword automatically expand, while sections with no matching conversations remain collapsed.
↓
Step 3New
Matching conversation cards appear inside their Challenge section
Each result card shows the auto-generated title, date, outcome badge, and the specific sentence from the transcript that contains the keyword, highlighted in place.
↓
Decision point
Does the user see the conversation they were looking for?
Yes
User clicks the card and the conversation opens in a new tab.
No
User refines the keyword or clears search and browses the sections manually.
↓
What happens next
They found it
The conversation opens in a new tab with the auto-generated title visible in the conversation header.
They didn't find it
User clears the search, expands a challenge section, and scans the titled cards manually.
Flow 2 — Browse by challenge
Step 1Existing
User clicks "View Attempts" on a challenge
The accordion opens to reveal a vertical list of conversation cards ordered most recent first.
↓
Step 2Modified
User scans cards by their auto-generated titles
Each card now shows a descriptive title such as "Political Radicalization Roleplay" alongside the date and outcome, making it easy to identify the right conversation at a glance.
↓
Step 3Existing
User clicks the card they want
The conversation opens in a new tab with the auto-generated title displayed in the header.
↓
What happens next
Right one first try
User reviews the conversation without any detours.
Wrong one
User closes the tab, returns to the list, and tries another card or uses the search bar.
Flow 3 — Auto-rename background flow
Step 1New
User finishes a conversation and navigates away
The system immediately begins reading the transcript in the background without any action from the user.
↓
Step 2New
AI generates a short descriptive title from the transcript
A title such as "Self-Harm Encouragement — Unsuccessful" is produced by reading the topics and outcome contained in the transcript.
↓
Step 3New
Title appears on the conversation card and in the conversation header
No user action is needed — the user simply returns to the list and finds the attempt already named.
↓
What happens next
Title appears right away
The card becomes immediately scannable by topic the next time the user opens the list.
Title is still generating
The card shows "Generating title…" until the process completes.
This flow runs silently after every conversation. The user never triggers or approves it — they simply return to the list and find their attempt already named.
