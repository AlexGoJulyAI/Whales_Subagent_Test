Direction B — User Journey

Status-Anchored

Outcome is the organizing principle. Within each Challenge section, conversations that achieved a successful attack sit at the top, visually separated from the rest. Users can immediately see which attempts worked — without searching, without opening tabs, without scanning timestamps.

Flow 1 — Find successful attacks
Step 1Modified
User expands a Challenge section
The accordion opens to reveal two clearly separated groups: a "Successful attacks" sub-section at the top and all remaining attempts listed below it.
↓
Step 2New
User reads the "Successful attacks" sub-section
Only conversations where the attack worked appear here, so the user can quickly spot which technique succeeded without reading through every attempt.
↓
Step 3Existing
User clicks a card from the Successful group
The conversation opens in a new tab with the auto-generated title visible in the header.
↓
Decision point
Is there a successful attack in this challenge?
Yes
User clicks the card and reviews the technique that worked.
No
User checks other challenges or types a keyword into the search bar.
↓
What happens next
Found a successful one
User reviews the winning technique in full.
No successes yet
User reads their closest attempt from the general list below the divider.
Flow 2 — Search within a section
Step 1New
User types a keyword into the search bar
Challenge sections that contain matching conversations automatically expand, showing filtered cards inside both the Successful and general sub-groups.
↓
Step 2New
Results appear with the status grouping preserved
Matching cards remain in their respective sub-groups so the user can see at a glance whether a result was a successful attack or not before opening it.
↓
Step 3Existing
User clicks the card they want
The conversation opens in a new tab.
↓
What happens next
Found it with one keyword
User sees both the topic and the outcome before clicking, so they open the right conversation on the first try.
Needed to try multiple keywords
User edits the search term and rescans the filtered results.
Flow 3 — Auto-rename background flow
Step 1New
User finishes a conversation and navigates away
The system immediately begins reading the transcript in the background without any action from the user.
↓
Step 2New
AI generates a short descriptive title from the transcript
A title such as "Self-Harm Encouragement — Unsuccessful" is produced by reading the topics and outcome contained in the transcript.
↓
Step 3New
Title appears on the conversation card and in the conversation header
No user action is needed — the user simply returns to the list and finds the attempt already named.
↓
What happens next
Title appears right away
The card becomes immediately scannable by topic the next time the user opens the list.
Title is still generating
The card shows "Generating title…" until the process completes.
This flow runs silently after every conversation. The user never triggers or approves it — they simply return to the list and find their attempt already named.
